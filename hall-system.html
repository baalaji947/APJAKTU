Why Hall-Based Scheduling Works

Advantages of moving students to subject-specific halls instead of bringing faculty to fixed classrooms.

Specialized Environments
Labs are equipped with domain-specific hardware and software. Lecture halls have large displays, tiered seating, and acoustic design. Students learn in rooms purpose-built for each discipline -- IoT labs have sensor kits, AI labs have GPU workstations, and seminar halls have multi-projector setups.
Resource Optimization
A single 120-seat seminar hall serves CS-A, CS-B, and CD-A for AI lectures in the morning, then CD-A and CD-B for Data Mining in the afternoon. Instead of equipping 6 classrooms with projectors and AV gear, the department invests in 10 high-quality rooms used at 76% average occupancy.
Cross-Section Interaction
When CS-A, CS-B, and CD-A students gather in Seminar Hall A for a common AI lecture, organic peer learning happens across section boundaries. Elective courses become natural mixing grounds where students from different specializations exchange perspectives and form cross-disciplinary study groups.
Flexible Scheduling
Rescheduling is as simple as reassigning a free hall to a displaced class. If Lecture Hall 2 needs maintenance, Software Testing can move to any available hall that hour. This decoupling of subject from room provides resilience against disruptions and simplifies last-minute timetable adjustments.
