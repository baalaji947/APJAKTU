The Problem with Current Internships

Why the Current System Fails

  • × Current S7/S8 internship is often a formality -- students are placed at companies with no meaningful work assigned to them
  • × Students receive "certificates" but gain no real, demonstrable skills they can show in interviews
  • × No portfolio pieces are created -- zero GitHub contributions, zero deployed projects, zero tangible output
  • × The college gains absolutely nothing from the process -- no systems built, no institutional improvement
  • × Companies treat interns as observers, not contributors -- leading to wasted time and disillusionment
65% of students report their internship was "not useful" in surveys

The Solution -- Two Tracks

Students choose the track that fits their goals. Both produce real, demonstrable outcomes.

Track A: Startup Partnership

Work on real products at real companies
  • ✓ Partner with 8-10 local tech startups in the region
  • ✓ Students work on real features and products, not toy exercises
  • ✓ 15-20 hours/week during S7 semester
  • ✓ Mentored by startup CTO + faculty advisor (dual mentorship)
  • ✓ Deliverable: Deployed feature + technical documentation

Example Projects

  • Build a customer analytics dashboard for a SaaS startup
  • Implement payment gateway integration (Razorpay/Stripe)
  • Create a mobile app module with React Native
  • Develop REST API endpoints for a fintech product

Track B: College Project Lab

Build systems the institution actually needs
  • ✓ Students build systems the college actually needs and will use
  • ✓ Faculty propose real problems, students architect and solve them
  • ✓ Teams of 3-4 students per project for collaborative experience
  • ✓ Deliverable: Working system + deployment + handover documentation

Example Projects

  • Automated Attendance System (using face recognition)
  • Smart Timetable Generator (constraint satisfaction)
  • Department Resource Portal (lesson plans, question banks)
  • Alumni Tracking Dashboard
  • Lab Equipment Booking System
  • Student Feedback Analytics Platform

Improvement Analysis

Who benefits and how -- a multi-stakeholder view of the proposed program.

For Students

  • Real portfolio with deployed projects to showcase in interviews
  • Meaningful GitHub history demonstrating consistent contributions
  • Reference letters from startup founders and CTOs
  • Genuine confidence in technical interviews backed by experience

For College

  • Working internal systems that reduce manual administrative work
  • Reduced dependency on expensive external software vendors
  • Showcaseable student projects for NAAC/NBA accreditation visits
  • Demonstrates innovation in pedagogy and industry collaboration

For Startups

  • Access to a curated talent pipeline of motivated students
  • Low-cost development capacity for feature building and testing
  • Potential future hires already trained on their tech stack
  • Fresh perspectives and energy applied to real product challenges

Implementation Plan

  • Pilot program with 20 students in S7 to validate the model
  • Expand to full batch participation by the following academic year
  • Dedicate 2 faculty members as full-time program coordinators
  • Establish formal MoUs with partner startups for accountability

Success Metrics

  • Track total deployment count across all student teams
  • Monitor GitHub activity -- commits, PRs, code reviews per student
  • Measure placement rate delta vs previous batch outcomes
  • Collect student satisfaction scores via structured feedback surveys

Proposed Timeline

A structured 7-month rollout from partner onboarding to final evaluation and demo day.

Phase 1
Month 1-2
Partner Onboarding & Project Scoping
Identify and onboard 8-10 startup partners. Define project scopes, expected deliverables, and mentorship commitments. Sign MoUs.
Phase 2
Month 3-4
Student Matching & Team Formation
Collect student interest profiles. Match students to tracks and projects based on skills and preferences. Form teams of 3-4.
Phase 3
Month 5-6
Active Development Sprints
Students work 15-20 hrs/week on assigned projects. Bi-weekly check-ins with mentors. Mid-point review and course correction.
Phase 4
Month 7
Demo Day, Handover & Evaluation
Final presentations to faculty and startup partners. System handover with documentation. Performance evaluation and certificates.
