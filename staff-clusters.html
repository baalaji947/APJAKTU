Key Insights

Cross-Branch Teaching
Semesters S1 and S2 are identical across CD (Data Science), CC (Cyber Security), and CS (Computer Science) branches. The same faculty cluster can teach Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Python, and C Programming across all three branches without any duplication of resources.
Coverage Gaps
12 subjects in the curriculum remain uncovered by current faculty expertise and require new hiring or adjunct support. These include Chemistry, Physics, Engineering Graphics, Electrical Engineering, Environmental Science, Constitution of India, and other foundational courses.
Methodology: Jaccard Similarity
Faculty are clustered using the Jaccard Similarity coefficient, which measures the overlap between two faculty members' subject sets as J(A,B) = |A intersect B| / |A union B|. Faculty pairs with similarity above the threshold (0.3) are grouped into the same cluster, ensuring domain coherence.
Optimization Benefits
Cluster-based allocation enables three key optimizations: (1) Load balancing by distributing subjects evenly within clusters, (2) Expertise matching by assigning high-expertise faculty to core courses, and (3) Gap identification by revealing uncovered subjects that require new faculty recruitment.
